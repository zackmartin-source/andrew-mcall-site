Book One · Available Now

The Knowledge of Good and Evil

In a garden full of permission, one tree was off limits. Most of us were taught it was a test of obedience. Andrew McCall believes it was something far more tender: a Father protecting the joy of giving His children everything Himself.

Across twelve chapters, this book follows that single thread through the whole of Scripture. The kings Israel demanded. The redeemer the law required. The confession Peter never could have reasoned his way into. Every page asks the same quiet question: where does your knowledge come from?

About the Author

The man behind the question

Andrew McCall is a theologian, teacher and former business owner who has spent his life at the intersection of deep study and everyday faith. He is not interested in giving you his opinions. He is interested in what the text has been saying all along.

When he is not writing, Andrew teaches, mentors and speaks on revelation knowledge: the simple, life-changing idea that God still wants to be the Source of everything His children know.

Years of study & ministry

How the books came to be

The Question

It began in seminary, with over a thousand methods of interpretation on the table and one verse that would not let go: why was the tree forbidden at all?

The Search

Years of study, ministry and honest wrestling. Notebooks filled with Hebrew word studies, from goel to imago Dei, all circling the same discovery.

The Answer

God never wanted His children to take knowledge from a tree. He wanted to give it Himself. Everything in Scripture flows from that one heartbeat.

The Books

A two-book series was born. The first traces the question through the Old Testament. The second follows it into the present, where the stakes are higher than ever.
